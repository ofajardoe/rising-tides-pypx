Evidence in Colombia

Migration due to climate changes is a reality. In Colombia, regions like Cartagena, Barranquilla, Santa Marta, and Juanchaco are extreme hotspots for this crisis.

Climate Displacement Factsheet (2016-2022)

  • 536,000 internal displacements were recorded due to disasters.
  • 2022 Fact: Unprecedented record year accounting for 281,000 displacements (52.4% of total).
  • Economic Impact: At-risk regions contribute to 76% of our GDP. Projected loss of 1.5% to 2.5% by 2050 without action.

Deep Research

Understanding the science behind rising sea levels and the profound impact on coastal communities.

🌍

1. The Science

The primary drivers of sea-level rise are thermal expansion (ocean water expands as it absorbs heat) and the melting of glaciers. As global temperatures increase, the oceans absorb over 90% of this excess heat.

🏘️

2. Vulnerability

In Colombia, cities like Cartagena and the Pacific coastal towns are experiencing accelerated coastal erosion. The intrusion of saltwater also threatens freshwater ecosystems and local agriculture.

🚶

3. Climate Refugees

When habitats become unlivable, communities are forced to migrate. These "climate refugees" often lack legal recognition. The social fabric of traditional coastal communities is torn apart as they relocate inland.

🛡️

4. Mitigation

Addressing this crisis requires Mitigation (reducing carbon footprints) and Adaptation (building resilient infrastructure and establishing planned relocation strategies).
